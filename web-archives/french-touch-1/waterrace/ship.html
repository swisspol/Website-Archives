WaterRace racing engines :

Like the rest of the game, the ships of WaterRace are also completely different from what you will find in any other racing game (with water or not).

 

WaterRace physic engine:

The physic engine in WaterRace is very detailed and realistic: each ship has over 50 unique physic parameters and the physic engine takes into account many physic forces. You're really driving an offshore racing boat!

Let's take an example: most ships are mainly directed using directional thrust, not the rudder (anyway the rudder only start being useful at high speeds because of the friction forces), so no thrust = no direction!
That's why it's pretty much difficult to control the ships at low speed i.e. under roughly 80Km/h. It's like in real life: offshore racing boats are not designed to be used at low speed and they behave very badly.

Another example: to take a turn successfully, you need to process as real offshore pilots do: just before the turn, cut the gaz (do not reverse, just cut), then turn fully to the left or the right, then when the ship is in the correct direction (or just before), set the gaz back to the max again. It works great!


Actually the physic engine is one of the most interesting feature of WaterRace: it's part of the challenge to learn how to drive the ships. It's definitely surprising for many people when playing the game for the first time because they think you can drive a ship as you would drive a car, but this is definitely not the case.

Hopefully, during the championship, the player has free access to a special training level where he can go to practice ship driving as many times and as long as he wants.
Typically, when the player has enough points to get access to a new ship and before using it in the championship, he should definitely spend some time in the training level to discover all the specifities of his new ship.

This is very rewarding for the player experience. While going further into the game, the player progressively becomes an experienced WaterRace offshore ship pilot.
Even hardcore racing games player will find a challenge in this game.

 

Input device:

Because of the physic, engine it's better to use the keyboard or a paddle to control the ships: you will be closer to the way of driving an offshore racing ship.

 

About the ships:

In most racing games, selecting another racing engine won't change a lot of things: you're still driving the same way you used to with the previous engine and you're just going faster or turning stronger.
It's not the case at all in WateRace: the ships are totally different! Each of them has a unique design, story and behaviour. Driving different ships give different feelings to the player. Really.

The ships pros and cons are carefully balanced so that no ship is able to win all the races (unless the player is very, very good). Faster ships are usually more difficult to drive or bigger (i.e. not well fitted for inshore levels like Venice), and slower ships might be easier to drive or smaller (you will be able to go under some bridges for example), but are... er... slower.

There is a real strategic aspect in the game: if you choose to race a given level using a ship that does not fit well the race design or that you don't really masterize, you might not get enough points to get to the next level, and it's the end of the championship - unless you have a Joker bonus item of course.

The parameters of the WaterRace ships (e.g. motor engine, size, weight...) are really close to the ones of real offshore racing boats. This means that they have realistic behaviour.
On the other hand, the look and physical appareance of most ships is quite unreal, but it fits perfectly WaterRace background story.

Regarding the arcade side of the game, each ship has a turbo engine, which you can turn on for a limited time only (i.e. a few seconds) provided you have picked up a Turbo bonus item previously. Most turbos are not directional and very powerful, which means that while the turbo is on, it's more difficult to turn. And while the turbo engine is on, you cannot turn it off anymore, so you better use it carefully and not before a strong turn!

Each ship is also provided with a set of eight totally different textures, each of them related to a WaterRace pilot. This is also great feature for network play!
